Pledging

Rights

  • To de-pledge at any time during the pledging process
  • To not be hazed
  • To not do anything that makes you feel uncomfortable
  • To know and ask questions about the requirements
  • To be granted alternative requirement options when appropriate
  • To have exceptions granted in the case of an extenuating circumstance
  • To express thoughts and concerns about the pledging process and its execution
  • To seek assistance if problems with other pledges or fraternity members arise
  • To be given warning when in danger of being de-pledged

Responsibilities

  • To prioritize school-work over pledging
  • To be respectful of all pledging materials, especially the pin
  • To fulfill all requirements on time (unless otherwise arranged)
  • To actively seek help when needed
  • To respect your fellow active members and pledges
  • To refrain from using drugs or alcohol during all Alpha Phi Omega events

Requirements

Fees

  • Pay all dues and fees
    • $100 due by MM/DD/YY
    • $35 due at Initiation

Attendance

  • Attend all pledge meetings
  • Attend Family Day
  • Attend IC Bonfire
  • Attend 2 fellowship events planned by the chapter
  • Attend 4 chapter committee meetings (your choice)
  • Attend 2 excecutive committee meetings
  • Attend 2 retreats
  • Attend 5 General Body Meetings

Hours and Interviews

  • Complete 16 hours of service
    • 5 max at any event
  • Interview all pledge brothers
  • Interview 80% of the active body (subactive limmitations)
  • Interview 2 IC brothers
  • One interview at a time
  • All interviews must be signed off

Pledge Pin

  • Must wear pledge pin at all times
  • Exceptions: sleep, sports, slosh, sex, and shower

Quizzes/Final

  • Pass all quizzes and final with 80% or better
  • Any failed quiz/final will be allowed 1 retake
  • Any failed quiz/final will result in 1 mark

Service

  • 1 service event for the pledge class
  • 1 service event for the entire chapter

Fellowship

  • 1 fellowship event for the pledge class
  • 1 fellowship event for the entire chapter
  • 2 chapter planned fellowship events

Treasury

  • Plan and fundraise $300

Plan and Attend Family Day

Each pledge is required to either chair or co-chair at least one event

Skit/Scrapbook

  • Create a pledge class scrapbook
  • Create a skit exemplifying the 3 principles of Alpha Phi Omega
  • Entire pledge class must be seen in the skit

Interviews

Interviews

  • Setting up an interview
    • Call or email only (no Facebook or text)
    • Being 15 minutes late will result in a mark
    • Be mindful of the time you call to set up an interview
  • During the interview
    • Minimum of 30 minutes
    • Actives and pledges have to sign off on interviews

Questions

  • Make sure your questions are appropriate
  • Be creative with your questions; this is your time to get to know your future brothers

Write Ups

  • All interviews must be posted on Tumblr
  • All interviews must contain a photo, 5 required facts, 3 random facts, and a paragraph about one of the random facts. An example is provided on the next page
  • A mock write-up is on our tumblr: http://apo-zo-alphaphi.tumblr.com/

Marks System

Marks System: You are allowed 7 marks until you are asked to leave. If you de-pledge becuase of marks, you are still allowed to pledge again during a different pledging time.

Ways to get a mark

  • Getting caught not wearing your pin
    • If your pin breaks, let the Pledge-Eds know ASAP
  • Not showing up to all the pledge meetings
    • Please make sure you are available to meet with your pledge class and Pledge-Eds weekly. Exceptions can be made with a good reason and a week's notice
  • Failure to meet the weekly deadlines
    • Please try to manage your time wisely. Meeting deadlines include pledge and active interviews with signatures, service hours, etc. For every deadline missed, you will get one mark
  • Not showing up to all mandatory general body meetings
    • Skit and toast song
  • Being more than 15 minutes late to an interview
  • Flaking on an interview
    • Being 15 minutes late to an interview without proper notice is considered flaking
    • If you need to reschedule an interview, please notify the active within 5 hours of the scheduled interview
  • Flaking on a service event without 24 hour notice
  • Failing a quiz (need to get a 80% or higher to pass)

Ways to get a mark off

  • Making a music video with your pledge class (note: this requires your class to have 100% involvement and attendance for this video to be considered)
  • Complete an extra 3 service hours for a service-hour checkpoint
  • Interview an alumni (namesake recommended)
  • Attend a LEADS workshop
  • Interview our advisor (Brian Self)
  • Plan and attend an additional pledge fellowship of service (all pledges must attend)
  • Go to 2 Mommy and Me Events

Things that will result in an automatic de-pledge

  • Consuming and/or being in the presence of alcohol while wearing your pin
  • Cheating on the quizzes or final
  • Reaching 7 marks

Pledge Exec Board Responsibilities

President

  • Oversees the other positions and makes sure that everyone is fulfilling their responsibilities
  • Aids any committee that may require extra help

VP Membership

  • Ensures that there are no problems between pledges and other members
  • Planning and completion of pledge skit on time

VP Fellowship

  • Planning and follow-through of pledge fellowship events

VP Service

  • Planning and follow-through of pledge service events

Treasurer

  • Plan the pledge fundraising event(s)
  • In charge of the money from fundraising events for the whole pledge class
  • Must raise at least $300, although more is recommended ($100 to the cause of your choice, $100 to the chapter, $100 to your choice)

Historian

  • Takes pictures at events
  • Ensures that the scrapbook is completed on time

Secretary

  • Takes notes during the pledge meetings
  • Makes sure pledge and actives (if applicable) know what they need to know after each meeting

While a number of pledges will be elected to these positions, it is absolutely not their responsibility to complete all of their tasks alone. Those without designated positions will be put into committees that will assist the elected members in completing their tasks. These positions were more or less designed to help make sure that pledge class requirements are completed on time and in a timely manner. Keep in mind that all pledges are expected to participate in each of these events.

Chapter Information Page

The Zeta Omicron Chapter of Alpha Phi Omega was charetd on May 24th, 1948. It was declared inactive in the 1980s due to low membership and was reactivated on June 6th, 1992.

In 1948 there were 31 students in the chartering group and 7 advisors led by president, Robert Kimball. In 1992 there were 24 students and 4 advisors in the re-chartering group led by president, William Overland.

Non-Mandatory Activities

Mommy and Me

Mommy and Me time is designed for pledges to get to know Tina and Victoria on a more personal level. They are weekly events that are just for the pledge educators and pledges. The dates and activities on the calendar are tentative and can be changed based on everyone's schedules.

Gold Star Incentive System

The gold-star system is used to encourage pledges to go above and beyond their requirements. We have provided a list of activities for pledges to do with actives and pledges.

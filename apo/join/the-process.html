The Process

Zeta Omicron is the name of our local chapter for Alpha Phi Omega. We call San Luis Obispo our home. We call Cal Poly our home. Joining our organization is reminiscent of the fraternity process. We hold rush events followed by a period of time called pledging. Successful completion of this process grants official membership. Thought not everyone will succeed, this process ensures that those that do are qualified people with like-minded aspirations. If interested, read on!

Rushing

Our rush week begins during the first week of the Fall and Spring quarters. We hold various events that preview the main activities of our chapter. Also, this is when we have our brothers actively showcasing Alpha Phi Omega in the UU or on Dexter Lawn. If you've never rushed a fraternity before, think of this as "testing the waters."

Pledging

If after you've tested the waters and you decided it was just right (pardon the pun), then this process would be like learning how to swim. While not much can be said about this quarter-long process (pledging is secret), two things are guaranteed. The very first guarantee is that there is no hazing whatsoever. It is nationally enforced and written in our bylaws. The second guarantee is that you will come out a better person. There are hardships, but speak to any brother, and they will say the same thing: "I'm glad I followed through."

Still Interested?

If genuinely curious or interested, please email us. We will send you reminders whenever we are holding our rush events. Your information will not be used for anything else. Also, we will answer all question related to our recruitment process and what Alpha Phi Omega stands for.
